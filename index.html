The Problem

Farming is one of the most stressful occupations in Ireland — and one of the hardest to get support for.

Isolation, financial pressure, unpredictable weather, and long working hours take a real toll. Yet research consistently shows that farmers rarely seek formal mental health support: services feel clinical, scheduling is impractical, and the stigma runs deep.

57% of Irish farmers in a 2018 Teagasc National Farm Survey reported experiencing stress or anxiety from farm work over the previous five years. Brennan et al. 2022, Journal of Agromedicine, 27(2)
Widespread isolation Research consistently identifies geographic isolation and loneliness as major stressors for Irish farming communities, particularly for younger and female farmers. Rose et al. 2023; Wheeler & Lobley 2022; O'Connor et al. 2025
A gap in provision We found limited visible evidence of programmes combining peer-led outdoor wellbeing support specifically designed for Irish farming communities. Based on our own desk research, 2026
How It Works

Three pillars. One goal: meet farmers where they already are.

🌿

Field Sessions

Fortnightly 60-minute outdoor wellbeing sessions on local farms, woodlands, and heritage sites. Led by trained peer Guides — active farmers who've completed our certification. Groups of 6–10. No clinic. No couch. Just land.

📱

The ZenFox App

Daily 5–15 minute audio sessions narrated by Irish farming voices. Seasonal 8-week "Resilience Trails." A nature observation journal called Fox Spotting. Designed for use in the field, the tractor, or the yard.

🤝

Community Network

Designed to work alongside existing farming organisations and community structures. Seasonal "Field Days" combine practical skills with guided outdoor mindfulness, normalising the practice within farming culture.

Related Research

Why we believe this approach has potential.

ZenFox itself is not yet clinically validated. But the principles behind it — nature-based mindfulness, peer-led support, and community integration — draw on a growing body of published research.

Nature-Based Mindfulness

A 2019 systematic review and meta-analysis found that nature-based mindfulness interventions produced statistically significant effects of medium size (g = 0.54), with wild and forest settings showing numerically larger effects than gardens.

Djernis et al., 2019, Int J Environ Res Public Health, 16(17)

Therapeutic Gardening Trial

A multi-site trial of 291 participants found medium-to-large effect sizes for therapeutic gardening on stress (d=0.90), anxiety (d=0.73), and loneliness (d=0.70). This study was conducted in Korea, not Ireland, so results may not directly transfer.

Kim et al., 2023, Nature Scientific Reports

Advisor Training Shows Promise

The "On Feirm Ground" programme trained Teagasc farm advisors in mental health awareness and significantly increased their knowledge and willingness to support farmers — evidence that trusted intermediaries can be effective.

Hammersley et al., 2024, J of Agric. Education & Extension

Farmers Prefer Informal Pathways

Multiple studies confirm that Irish and UK farmers prefer community-based, peer-led support over clinical settings. Effective services must accommodate erratic schedules and avoid stigmatising language.

Brennan et al., 2024, J of Rural Studies; Rose et al. 2024
Important: The research above supports the general principles behind ZenFox's design but does not directly validate ZenFox as a product. We intend to conduct our own pilot evaluation using validated measures (WHO-5, GAD-7) before making claims of effectiveness. ZenFox is a wellbeing support service, not a medical treatment or substitute for professional mental health care.
The App

A pocket companion for the fields

Designed for 5-bar signal or none. Built for muddy hands and early mornings.

🎧

Audio Sessions

5–15 min guided practices narrated by farming voices. Downloadable for offline use in areas with poor connectivity.

🌱

Resilience Trails

8-week guided programmes aligned with the seasons: calving stillness, harvest focus, winter rest.

🦊

Fox Spotting

A nature observation journal. Log wildlife, weather, and landscapes as a daily mindfulness anchor.

🛡️

Signposting to Support

Optional wellbeing check-ins with clear signposting to established helplines and services if someone needs more than peer support.
