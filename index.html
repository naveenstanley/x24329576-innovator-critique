The Problem

Farming is one of the most stressful occupations in Ireland — and the hardest to get help for.

Isolation, financial pressure, unpredictable weather, and 80-hour weeks take a profound toll. Yet farmers rarely seek support: services feel clinical, scheduling is impossible, and the stigma runs deep.

57% of Irish farmers have experienced significant anxiety in the last five years, with isolation the primary amplifier.
4× Women farmers with high geographic isolation stress are four times more likely to experience depression than men.
Zero nature-based, peer-led mindfulness programmes currently exist tailored to Irish farming communities.
How It Works

Three pillars. One mission: meet farmers where they already are.

🌿

Field Sessions

Fortnightly 60-minute outdoor sessions on local farms, woodlands, and heritage sites. Led by trained peer Guides — active farmers who've completed our certification. Groups of 6–10. No clinic. No couch. Just land.

📱

The ZenFox App

Daily 5–15 minute audio sessions narrated by Irish farming voices. Seasonal 8-week "Resilience Trails." A nature observation journal called Fox Spotting. Built for use in the field, the tractor, or the yard.

🤝

Community Network

Integrated with IFA branches, Teagasc offices, and Macra na Feirme. Seasonal "Field Days" combine practical skills with guided mindfulness, normalising the practice within farming culture.

The Evidence

Nature-based mindfulness works. The research is clear.

We didn't invent this. We're bringing decades of clinical evidence to the people who need it most — in the setting where it works best.

Medium-to-Large Effect Sizes

A 2019 meta-analysis found nature-based mindfulness interventions produced statistically significant therapeutic effects (g = 0.54), with wild/forest settings outperforming gardens and parks.

Djernis et al., Int J Environ Res Public Health

Stress Reduction: d = 0.90

A multi-site trial of therapeutic gardening among 291 participants showed large effect sizes across depression (0.58), anxiety (0.73), stress (0.90), and loneliness (0.70).

Kim et al., 2023, Nature Scientific Reports

Peer-Led Models Work

The "On Feirm Ground" programme trained Teagasc farm advisors and significantly increased their knowledge, capacity to identify poor wellbeing, and willingness to support farmers.

Hammersley et al., 2024, J of Agricultural Extension

Farmers Prefer Informal Support

Research confirms Irish farmers prefer community-based, peer-led pathways over clinical settings. Services must accommodate erratic schedules and avoid stigmatising language.

Brennan et al., 2024, Journal of Rural Studies
