# ZenFox — Business Plan & Marketing Campaign

## Executive Summary

ZenFox is an Irish social enterprise delivering nature-based mental resilience programmes to farming communities. The service combines peer-led outdoor mindfulness sessions, a mobile app with farming-voiced audio content, and integration with trusted agricultural institutions (IFA, Teagasc, Macra na Feirme). ZenFox addresses the validated gap between strong evidence for nature-based mindfulness therapy and the near-total absence of culturally appropriate, stigma-free mental health services for Irish farmers.

---

## Market Opportunity

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- 135,000 active farms in Ireland (CSO 2020)
- ~300,000 people directly involved in farming operations
- €1.2B annual cost of mental health-related lost productivity in Irish agriculture (estimated from HSE data and EU farm advisory reports)

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
- 40,000 farmers aged 25–55 in high-isolation counties showing moderate stress symptoms
- Plus 15,000 farm family members (spouses, adult children) affected by farm stress

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1
- 800 farmers across 4 pilot counties (West Cork, Kerry, Roscommon, Mayo)
- 80 trained ZenFox Guides
- 2,500 app subscribers

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Seeds (Months 1–6)
- Recruit founding Guide cohort (20 farmers) from IFA Mental Health ambassadors
- Deliver pilot Field Sessions in West Cork and Kerry
- Soft-launch app (free tier) to 500 farmers through Teagasc advisor network
- Secure HSE pilot funding commitment

### Phase 2: Growth (Months 7–18)
- Expand to 4 additional counties (Roscommon, Mayo, Donegal, Leitrim)
- Launch premium app tier with seasonal Resilience Trails
- First corporate sponsorship deals (FBD Insurance, Dairygold)
- Publish pilot outcomes data (pre/post validated mental health measures)

### Phase 3: Scale (Months 19–36)
- National rollout: 20 counties, 200+ Guides
- UK expansion (Wales, Scotland, Northern England — similar farming demographics)
- Enterprise tier: Teagasc, co-ops, and agri-businesses buy bulk licences for their farming members
- API integration with farm management software (Herdwatch, Kingswood)

## Marketing Campaign: "Still Ground"

### Campaign Concept
*"Still Ground"* — a double meaning: the quiet earth beneath a farmer's feet, and the act of standing still on ground you know intimately. The campaign normalises mindfulness as a farming skill, not a wellness trend.

### Channels & Tactics

**1. Partner Distribution (Primary — 50% of acquisition)**
- IFA newsletter features and branch-level introductions
- Teagasc advisor referral cards (physical cards left after farm visits)
- Macra na Feirme event sponsorships and workshops
- Co-op notice boards and mart bulletin boards

**2. Earned Media (25% of awareness)**
- RTÉ Nationwide / Ear to the Ground feature pitch
- Irish Farmers' Journal profile piece
- Irish Times Health supplement (farm mental health angle)
- AgriLand and That's Farming digital coverage
- Podcast appearances: The Farming Independent, Nuffield Ireland

**3. Community Events (15% of acquisition)**
- "Still Ground" demonstration sessions at National Ploughing Championships
- Pop-up sessions at local agricultural shows (Tullamore Show, Bonniconlon)
- Partnership with Men's Sheds Ireland for cross-referral

**4. Digital (10% of acquisition)**
- Instagram/TikTok: short-form video series "2 Minutes of Stillness" — filmed on real Irish farms, no narration, just ambient sound. Each ends with ZenFox branding.
- Facebook community groups for farming counties (targeted, organic)
- Google Ads: "farmer stress support Ireland" keyword cluster
- App Store Optimization for "farming mindfulness" and "rural mental health"

### Key Metrics
- Waitlist signups (pre-launch): 2,000
- Pilot programme completion rate: >70%
- App 30-day retention: >40%
- NPS score: >60
- Media mentions: 15+ in first 6 months

## Impact Measurement

ZenFox measures outcomes using validated instruments:
- **WHO-5 Well-Being Index** (primary)
- **GAD-7** (anxiety)
- **PHQ-9** (depression)
- **UCLA Loneliness Scale** (social isolation)
- **Psychological Flexibility Questionnaire** (ACT-based)

All measures taken at baseline, 8 weeks, 6 months, and 12 months. Anonymised, GDPR-compliant. Reported annually to funders and published as open-access research.

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Gap ZenFox Fills |
|-----------|------------------|
| Headspace / Calm | Not farming-specific; not peer-led; no in-person component; clinical framing |
| On Feirm Ground (Teagasc) | Advisor training only — doesn't deliver direct intervention to farmers |
| Farmaffinity | Counselling service — clinical, appointment-based, not nature-embedded |
| Pieta House | Crisis intervention — not prevention or resilience building |
| Rural Support NI | Northern Ireland only; phone-based; no nature/mindfulness component |

**ZenFox is the only solution combining:** nature-based settings + peer-led delivery + mobile app companion + farming-calendar alignment + non-clinical framing.

## Why Now

1. **Policy tailwind:** Ireland's Healthy Ireland framework and HSE mental health reform both prioritise community-based, preventive interventions.
2. **Cultural shift:** Post-COVID farming community has become more open to mental health conversations (On Feirm Ground, IFA campaigns).
3. **Evidence maturity:** Nature-based mindfulness research has reached critical mass — enough RCTs and meta-analyses to satisfy institutional funders.
4. **Technology readiness:** Offline-first mobile apps, low-bandwidth audio streaming, and React Native make the technical build straightforward.
5. **No incumbent:** Despite all the above, nobody has built this specific solution for this specific population. The gap is wide open.
