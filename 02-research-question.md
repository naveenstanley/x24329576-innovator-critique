# Research Question

**Derived from:** *The Fox of Glendalough* — a story about wild nature, communal stillness, cross-species calm, and the rediscovery of ancient contemplative practices in Irish rural landscapes.

---

## Primary Research Question

**To what extent can structured, nature-based mindfulness programmes delivered in Ireland's rural heritage landscapes reduce chronic stress and anxiety among isolated farming communities, and what design principles maximise sustained engagement in populations traditionally resistant to mental-health interventions?**

### Sub-questions

1. What is the current prevalence and economic burden of untreated stress, anxiety, and social isolation among Irish farmers and rural agricultural workers?
2. How do nature-immersive settings (heritage sites, ancient woodlands, rewilded landscapes) modulate therapeutic outcomes compared to indoor clinical environments for this demographic?
3. What cultural, social, and identity-related barriers prevent Irish farming communities from accessing conventional mental-health support, and how might programme design overcome these barriers?
4. Can animal-assisted or nature-encounter elements (e.g., wildlife observation, working-animal bonding) serve as engagement anchors that reduce dropout in rural mindfulness interventions?
